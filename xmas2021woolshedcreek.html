Xmas 2021

Woolshed Creek Hut

Dec 19-20, 2021

Our holidays started on the 17th, which was a Friday - yes, the schools in NZ finished the semester before the end of the week (some even two days earlier). Iza was so excited that she started packing right away, even though we weren't leaving until Saturday and thus had the whole day to pack. She followed aunty Meg's awesome packing list for tramping, colouring the items as she squared them away in the bag (many of the items didn't get coloured, because dad said he'll pack them).

Day 1

The trek to the Woolshed Creek Hut, our first tramp of the vacation, started Sunday after a night spent in a holiday park in Timaru. We were joined by our Dunedin friends Masha and Maxim (who happened to book the same hut, completely independently of us, for the same night) and their two kids: Liova (6) and Yeva (3).

We set off at noon, starting with a fairly steep climb along the Rhyolite Track. We took our time with plenty of stops along the way.

And so, even though it should have taken 1h, we made it to the top of the ridge after 2h.

For a moment, the path remained flat along a tussock plain...

...but soon enough there were more rocks and a lot of going up and down. Liova and Iza tramped like troopers, fuelled by stories.

It took us 6h to get to the hut. We only had strength for a quick dinner and then went to bed.

Day 2

The next day it was quite windy. No issue for Masha's family, as they planned to stay the second night in the hut. We were advised (by a fellow tramper) to walk out along a 4-wheel track that eventually joined the easy, eastward path to the carpark. This was on the account of the wind and its potential to blow a child of Iza's size off the ridge. We followed the advice and the 4-wheel detour, holding hands and braving the winds. At one point we sheltered from the gale in an old mine.

But overall, the easy track turned out to be super easy, and we made it out in just over 2h. The next item on our tramping agenda: a night at a motel in Lewis Pass before venturing onto the Lake Daniell track, where we were hoping to meet up with the Pakburgs.

Tramp info

The track to the Woolshed Creek Hut is a loop, with the eastward side being a bit harder, but more picturesque. The distance from the Carpark to the hut is around 6km on each side, and despite the difference in difficultly, time-wise each is supposed to take around 3h. We took the the harder side on the way to the hut, returning along the westward side, with a small detour along the 4-wheel drive road for the first couple of kms.
